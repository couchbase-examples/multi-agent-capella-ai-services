# Seinfeld Episode: George thinks that he is losing his memory

---

### Episode Title: "The Forgetful Universe"

---

**COLD OPEN**

**INT. JERRY'S APARTMENT - DAY**

(JERRY is on the couch, rummaging through the sofa cushions for the TV remote. Just as he finds it, GEORGE bursts in, looking frantic.)

**GEORGE:**
Jerry, I can't find my car. It's gone! Disappeared into the void!

**JERRY:**
Here we go. First, it was the keys; now it's the car. Next, you'll need a GPS for your thoughts!

**GEORGE:**
I'm serious, Jerry. This could be it. The beginning of... (shudders) memory loss!

**JERRY:**
(smiling) George Costanza, the man who forgot his own paranoia! You know, even mailmen lose letters, but they don’t diagnose themselves with cognitive decline.

(KRAMER enters energetically, holding a bizarre-looking contraption.)

**KRAMER:**
You guys are not gonna believe this! I found the solution—(points to contraption) the Memory Enhancer 3000. Never forget anything again!

**JERRY:**
(to camera) Well, nothing's foolproof when it comes to Kramer.

(Cuts to Elaine entering, noticing GEORGE's distress.)

**ELAINE:**
George, what's your crisis now?

**GEORGE:**
Elaine, I'm losing my memory!

**ELAINE:**
(folding arms) George, how are you managing all this chaos over forgetting one tiny detail?

(KRAMER excitedly aims his contraption at GEORGE.)

**KRAMER:**
Prepare to have your mind...remembered!

(The machine whirs loudly, causing lights to flicker and everyone to jump back. Freeze frame as theme music plays.)

---

**ACT ONE**

**INT. JERRY'S APARTMENT - DAY**

(JERRY and GEORGE are discussing the events over coffee.)

**GEORGE:**
Jerry, I read about these memory tests. It said if you forget where you parked, it's a sign.

**JERRY:**
Y'know, George, if forgetting where your car is means the onset of madness, we're all doomed. Fuhgeddaboutit!

**INT. MONK'S COFFEE SHOP - DAY**

(GEORGE sits at the counter, scribbling paranoid notes on a small pad, with ELANE next to him.)

**GEORGE:**
I’m writing down everything I do, step by step. Can't let anything slip.

**ELAINE:**
(mockingly) Well, are you writing halfway through? We all forget things, George—like me, forgetting your melodrama.

**INT. HEALTH FAIR BOOTH - DAY**

(GEORGE stands nervously in front of a HEALTH FAIR ATTENDANT.)

**HEALTH FAIR ATTENDANT:**
Sir, this is just a routine memory test. Relax.

**GEORGE:**
(panic rising) Routine? I'll have you know, nothing routine happens to George Costanza!

**INT. JERRY'S APARTMENT - NIGHT**

(KRAMER is enthusiastically explaining a "brain diet" to GEORGE, shoveling nuts and berries into a bowl.)

**KRAMER:**
It's about nutrients, George! You gotta feed the thinker.

**GEORGE:**
(poking at the bowl skeptically) Nuts and seeds? I'm supposed to remember through squirrel food?

---

**ACT TWO**

**INT. ELAINE'S OFFICE - DAY**

(ELAINE is on the phone, frustrated. GEORGE has forgotten to pick her up.)

**ELAINE:**
(rebuking to GEORGE) Forget it, George. Then again, that's your specialty now, isn't it?

**INT. MONK'S COFFEE SHOP - DAY**

(The group is trying to figure out the mixed-up personal items due to KRAMER’s contraption.)

**JERRY:**
Ladies and gentlemen, another successful operation...to confuse us all.

**KRAMER:**
Defective signal fluctuations, Jerry. Simple misdirect, everything's under control!

**GEORGE:**
What isn't these days?

**INT. ELAINE'S FRIEND’S PARTY - DAY**

(ELAINE struggles to remember someone’s name.)

**ELAINE:**
Hey...you! You’re, uh...

**FRIEND:**
(sighs) It's okay, Elaine. It happens to everyone.

**ELAINE:**
(to herself) George's forgetfulness is infectious, apparently.

---

**FINALE**

**INT. JERRY'S APARTMENT – NIGHT**

(JERRY stands circling the room, summing up the episode while the others sit.)

**JERRY:**
Maybe we're not losing memory, just reprioritizing...like cleaning out a closet.

(GEORGE nods, finally calm, as KRAMER's device unexpectedly activates, sending a cascade of lights and sounds.)

**KRAMER:**
Ah, I might've...miscalculated again.

(As everyone scurries around, the room plunges into comedic chaos, concluding with GEORGE standing still, oddly peaceful.)

**GEORGE:**
Eh, maybe forgetting isn't so bad after all.

(Eventual fade-out as JERRY shrugs at the camera.)

---

### TAG SCENE

**INT. MONK'S COFFEE SHOP - DAY**

(JERRY finishes recounting the incident to the group.)

**GEORGE:**
So that's it? Memory's just a series of unprompted moments?

**JERRY:**
(pensively) Sort of... like a lost left sock.

**KRAMER:**
(bursting in) The universe of the lost socks!

(Everyone chuckles, signaling a close.)

This episode works well in maintaining Seinfeld's core themes of observational humor and exaggerated neuroticism. The script captures the authentic voices and dynamics of the characters, particularly George's tendency to escalate minor inconveniences into crises and Jerry's ability to turn these incidents into comedic anecdotes. The integration of Kramer's eccentricity and the group dynamic strengthens the plot, further enriched by callbacks to typical Seinfeld-style misunderstandings. The episode length, pacing, and scene transitions remain tight and in line with classic Seinfeld.
